Executive Summary

Resolving the Strategic Tension

This proposal addresses a fundamental strategic tension in Special Operations Force employment: the competing demands of internal security responsiveness versus territorial defense preparation.

The establishment of an FSRR-SOCOM satellite training center at Camp Kibaritan Military Reservation resolves this dilemma by creating a forward-positioned capability that simultaneously addresses both missions.

The Problem

Internal Security Reality: Mindanao remains vulnerable to extremist fragmentation, insurgent reconstitution, and transnational threats requiring precision SOF response. Current Luzon-based positioning creates 12-24 hour deployment friction.

Territorial Defense Imperative: Under evolving defense posture, SOF must prepare for strategic reconnaissance, deep penetration operations, and near-peer conditions — missions requiring mountain proficiency, austere environment training, and geographic redundancy that Luzon cannot fully provide.

The Solution

Dual-Mission Posture: Camp Kibaritan provides central Mindanao positioning enabling 30-90 minute rotary-wing response to any Infantry Division AO, while maintaining highland terrain (500-1,100m elevation) that replicates territorial defense operating environments.

Training-Posture Integration: The 42,000-hectare reservation supports mountain warfare, airborne operations, battalion-scale maneuver, and austere reconnaissance training — ensuring forces remain tactically sharp for internal security while building competencies for external defense missions.

Strategic Preservation: By establishing a dedicated training infrastructure separate from division control, SOF maintains operational independence and prevents tactical consumption, preserving strategic-level capabilities for territorial defense while remaining responsive to internal security requirements.

Recommendation: Approve development of FSRR-SOCOM satellite training center at Zone 8, Camp Kibaritan Military Reservation, enabling a force multiplier capability that reconciles immediate operational needs with long-term strategic requirements within the Comprehensive Archipelagic Defense Concept.

The Kibaritan Solution

The Design Logic of Camp Kibaritan

Camp Kibaritan Military Reservation resolves this dilemma by integrating posture and training.

Central Positioning for Rapid Response

From Bukidnon: Rotary-wing response to any Mindanao Division in 30–90 minutes • Reinforcement of hotspots without Luzon deployment delays • Persistent SOF presence without tactical over-commitment

→ This preserves responsiveness to internal security

Terrain That Enables Dual-Mission Training

Surrounded by Mt. Kitanglad (2,899m), Mt. Kalatungan (2,860m), Mt. Piapayungan (2,085m) providing jungle warfare terrain, high-altitude cold-weather exposure, extended ridgeline maneuver corridors, concealed reconnaissance routes, and natural long-range observation platforms.

No other single military reservation offers this full-spectrum terrain set domestically.

→ This terrain simultaneously supports Internal Security readiness AND Territorial Defense preparation — without doctrinal compromise

Training for Distributed Operations

Modern SOF operations require decentralized execution, sustainment under isolation, long-duration field operations, and communications in degraded environments.

The 42,000-hectare area enables multi-day reconnaissance exercises, dispersion drills, redundant C2 node experimentation, and force survivability training.

→ This directly supports external defense modernization

Avoiding Mission Drift

By establishing a satellite camp — not staging FSRR line units semi-permanently under a single JTF — we preserve strategic autonomy of SOF, prevent habitual tactical consumption, maintain training calendar discipline, and balance operational support with modernization.

→ Thus, SOF remains a strategic asset — not a purely reactive force

Strategic Dispersion and Survivability

Concentration of elite forces in Luzon creates vulnerability. Distributed posture between Luzon and Mindanao creates geographic redundancy, force survivability, reconstitution capacity, and alternate command potential.

Camp Kibaritan's elevation and interior location reduce exposure to maritime first-strike vectors. This supports national resilience.

Section 05

Camp Kibaritan Reservation Boundary

The Camp Kibaritan Military Reservation was established under Proclamation No. 134, s. 1963, signed by President Diosdado Macapagal, reserving parcels of public domain in the barangays of Malinao, Kibaritan, and Bacoganon in what is now Kalilangan, Bukidnon, along with portions extending into Lanao del Sur.

The reservation encompasses approximately 42,000 hectares of varied terrain — from highland plateaus suitable for airborne operations and drop zones, to dense jungle canopy ideal for ranger and unconventional warfare training. Elevation ranges from approximately 500m to 1,100m ASL, providing diverse training environments within a single installation.

Proposed Training Capabilities

The satellite camp supports SOCOM's core mission sets across three training pillars — leveraging the reservation's terrain for both internal security and external defense training.

Scout Sniper Course

Conduct the Scout Sniper Course within the reservation, exploiting its diverse terrain — from highland plateaus to dense jungle canopy — to train precision marksmen in field craft, long-range engagement, stalking, observation, and target interdiction.

Airborne Operations

Establish designated Drop Zones within the reservation's highland plateaus for static-line and freefall parachute training, supporting airborne qualification and sustainment jumps.

Jungle Warfare Center

Exploit the reservation's dense tropical canopy for advanced jungle operations training including survival, evasion, resistance, and escape (SERE) exercises.

Politically Sensitive Site Reconnaissance

The reservation's proximity to the Bukidnon–Lanao del Sur–Maguindanao corridor provides operationally realistic training scenarios for special reconnaissance toward politically sensitive and potentially hostile sites , including simulated approaches to MILF/MNLF camps and autonomous region boundaries.

Joint/Combined Training

Rotating SRBns can conduct joint training exercises with Infantry Division organic units through 1st Training Group, enhancing interoperability between SOCOM elements and conventional forces in Mindanao.

Ready Reaction Force

Maintain a forward-deployed Scout Ranger element capable of rapid deployment to any threat area in Mindanao within 60 minutes by rotary-wing aircraft.

Integrated Impact Area & Combined/Joint Firing Ranges

The reservation's 42,000-hectare expanse provides sufficient area to establish dedicated integrated impact areas and combined/joint firing ranges that support simultaneous live-fire exercises across multiple weapons systems — from individual sniper engagements to crew-served weapons and mortar fire.

External & Territorial Defense Training

CKMR's mountainous terrain replicates the topography of border regions and contested territories, enabling SOCOM to conduct realistic training scenarios that mirror special reconnaissance missions toward borders of potential adversaries — including infiltration routes, observation post establishment, and long-duration surveillance in denied areas.

High-Altitude & Cold-Weather Operations

Leveraging the adjacent Mt. Kitanglad (2,899m), Mt. Kalatungan (2,860m), and Mt. Piapayungan (2,085m) ranges for acclimatization, mountain warfare, and austere environment training at temperatures reaching 5–10°C — replicating conditions for potential cross-border and archipelagic defense operations in varied elevation zones.

Section 08

National Defense Posture & Continuity

Beyond training, Camp Kibaritan serves as a critical national defense asset — contributing to force preservation, strategic depth, and continuity of operations in a full-spectrum conflict scenario.

Alternate Command & Control Center

In the event of a full-blown armed conflict — particularly one involving strikes against national command infrastructure in Luzon — the concentration of AFP and Philippine Army headquarters assets in the National Capital Region represents a critical single point of failure. Camp Kibaritan's remote highland location in central Mindanao, far from coastlines and major urban centers, makes it an ideal alternate command and control (C2) site for both the Philippine Army and the AFP.

The reservation's vast 42,000-hectare area provides ample space for dispersed C2 nodes, communications infrastructure, and supporting elements that would be extremely difficult for an adversary to target. Its proximity to multiple APOD/SPODs ensures that the alternate C2 center can maintain connectivity with deployed forces across the archipelago.

Continuity of Government (COG) & Continuity of Operations (COOP)

National defense planning requires designated Continuity of Government (COG) and Continuity of Operations (COOP) sites that can sustain government and military functions if primary installations in Luzon are degraded or denied. Camp Kibaritan satisfies the key criteria for a COG/COOP site:

Geographic separation — over 900 km from NCR, beyond the effective range of most conventional strike systems targeting Luzon.
Terrain protection — highland elevation (500–1,100m ASL) surrounded by mountain ranges provides natural concealment and defense-in-depth.
Existing legal reservation — Proclamation No. 134 already designates the land for military purposes, enabling immediate development without land acquisition delays.
Multi-modal access — proximity to aerial and sea ports enables strategic lift for government continuity operations and evacuation procedures.

Rearward Staging for Reserve SOF Forces

Current SOCOM force posture concentrates the Philippines' most elite special operations forces — the FSRR, Special Forces Regiment (Airborne), and Light Reaction Regiment — primarily in Luzon-based installations (Camp Tecson, Fort Magsaysay). In a conflict scenario involving a near-peer adversary, these Luzon-based assets face immediate exposure to preemptive strikes, particularly from standoff weapons targeting known military installations.

Camp Kibaritan provides an ideal rearward location for maintaining reserve SOF forces that are shielded from the immediate threat envelope facing Luzon. By pre-positioning trained SOF elements in Mindanao, the AFP ensures that even in a worst-case scenario where Luzon-based forces are degraded, a reconstitution capability exists — preserving the nation's ability to conduct unconventional warfare, special reconnaissance, and direct action operations from a secure rear area.

Force Preservation & Strategic Depth

The principle of strategic depth — maintaining critical military capabilities beyond the immediate reach of an adversary — is fundamental to Philippine defense planning under the Comprehensive Archipelagic Defense Concept (CADC). Establishing a SOCOM satellite camp at CKMR creates a second echelon of elite forces that can:

Absorb and reconstitute — receive evacuated SOF personnel from threatened Luzon installations and rapidly reconstitute operational units.
Sustain UW capability — maintain the AFP's unconventional warfare capacity even if primary SOF bases are neutralized.
Project force southward — defend the southern Philippines and maintain sovereignty over Mindanao, the Sulu Archipelago, and the Celebes Sea approaches.
Support allied operations — serve as a staging area for combined operations with allied SOF under mutual defense treaty obligations.

Implementation Roadmap

Phase 1 — Assessment & Coordination (Months 1–6)

Joint field reconnaissance with IMCOM . Stakeholder engagement at Army and GHQ levels. Secure DND endorsement and funding.

Phase 2 — Infrastructure Development (Months 6–18)

Construct cantonment core: HQ/TOC, barracks, helipad, ranges, and training facilities per the proposed site selection plan.

Phase 3 — Initial Operating Capability (Months 18–24)

Deploy initial cadre. Commence scout sniper and SRBn proficiency training courses. Establish comms and begin coordinated training with 4ID.

Phase 4 — Full Operating Capability (Months 24–36)

Full training capability including airborne ops. Permanent ready-reaction force element. Joint exercises with all Mindanao Infantry Divisions.

Conclusion

The Strategic Imperative

Camp Kibaritan Military Reservation's central position in Mindanao, its 42,000-hectare training area, proximity to three mountain ranges, adjacency to aerial and sea ports, suitability as an alternate C2/COOP site, and its existing legal status under Proclamation No. 134 make it the optimal site for the FSRR–SOCOM satellite training camp — transforming SOCOM's posture from reactive to proactive.

Requested Actions
